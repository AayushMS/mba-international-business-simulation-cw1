## Scene 6: Task 2 -- Deep Analysis
**Voice:** Ava (Voice 2) then Andrew (Voice 1) | **Duration:** 87.1s total
**Files:** `scene06_analysis_ava.mp3` (43.0s), `scene06_analysis_andrew.mp3` (44.1s)

### Ava's Part:

> Task two is where we went deep -- thirteen frameworks across four dimensions. V.R.I.O. tested which resources give real advantage. Atlanta's learning curve? Temporary at best. Vietnam's low costs? Shared with competitors, so just parity. Value Chain showed Just-In-Time works for predictable four-G demand but breaks down for uncertain five-G volumes. Then strategic positioning. The BCG Matrix made it crystal clear -- four-G is the cash cow funding everything, five-G is a question mark with zero market share. And Bowman's Strategy Clock shows Mo-bee-lay migrating from a hybrid position to focused differentiation as it moves through the waves.

### Andrew's Part:

> Externally, Porter's Five Forces confirmed intense rivalry -- four equal firms in direct price competition post-DOJ. Critical Success Factors identified the biggest gap: Mo-bee-lay's complete absence from five-G. That's not minor, it's existential. Now here's what the international strategy frameworks revealed. Bartlett and Ghoshal shows Mo-bee-lay needs to shift from global standardization for four-G to a transnational strategy for five-G and A.I., balancing integration with local responsiveness across the US, Europe, and Asia. Entry Mode Analysis points to licensing for fast five-G entry, partnerships for A.I. Thirteen frameworks, one message: the window is open, but closing fast.

## Scene 8: Task 4 -- Strategic Recommendation
**Voice:** Andrew (Voice 1) | **Duration:** 55.1s
**File:** `scene08_recommendation.mp3`

> Our recommendation: Accelerated Five-G Entry with Parallel A.I. R and D, Funded by Managed Four-G Transition. For four-G, defend the cash engine but accept managed decline. Shift production to Vietnam, accept share dropping from twenty-five to twenty-two percent, and extract cash to fund everything else. For five-G, license technology for fast market entry within six to nine months. Yes, licensing creates dependency, but speed beats purity right now. Build proprietary I.P. in parallel for the second-gen product. For A.I., commit one hundred fifty million to R and D. Dedicated team. Strategic partnerships. Prototype by Q four twenty twenty-seven. And honestly? We can't do everything at maximum scale. The ninety million dollar floor means sequencing investments carefully. This is real strategy, not wishful thinking.
