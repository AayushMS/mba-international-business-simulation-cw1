# Mobilé Inc. — Production Facilities

## USA Plant: Atlanta, Georgia

### Capacity
- 12 production lines
- Cost: USD 2.9M per new line

### Labour
- Cost: ~USD 28/hour
- Deep accumulated learning-curve efficiencies from years of operation

### Primary Role
- Serves US domestic market
- Exports to Europe

### Key Risk
- High labour costs erode competitiveness on cost-sensitive products
- Premium/mid-premium products remain viable

## Asia Plant: Vietnam

### Capacity
- Growing facility (case study does not specify current number of production lines — unlike Atlanta's stated 12 lines)
- Cost: USD 3.3M per new line (higher per-line cost than Atlanta; likely reflects newer construction + more advanced capability)

### Labour
- Cost: ~USD 6/hour (lowest of all three regions)
- Significant cost advantage over Atlanta

### Primary Role
- Serves Asian market at zero tariff
- Exports to Europe via EU-Vietnam Free Trade Agreement (EVFTA)

### Key Risk
- US-Vietnam trade tensions create tariff uncertainty
- Potential ~USD 12+/unit tariff on Asia→USA exports

### Strategic Value
- Samsung, Intel, and LG all operate major facilities in Vietnam (validation of location)
- Structural logistics advantage for Asian market
- EVFTA provides European export route

## Production Rules & Constraints

### Just-In-Time (JIT)
- No finished goods stockpiling
- Production matches demand forecasts precisely

### Penalties
- **Overproduction:** 5-10% cost penalty
- **Underproduction:** Permanently lost sales (cannot recover)

### New Plant Lead Time
- **2 years** from investment decision to operational availability
- Capacity committed today arrives in Year 3 — precisely when demand is expected to peak
- This decision CANNOT be deferred

### Contract Manufacturing
- Available as alternative to owned production
- Cost premium: 15-20% above in-house
- Disadvantage: No learning-curve benefit
- Use case: Bridge capacity gaps while waiting for new lines

| Line cost | $2.9M | $3.3M |
| Labour/hour | $28 | $6 |
| Learning curve | Deep (years of ops) | Building |
| Domestic market | USA | Asia |
| Export to Europe | Direct | Via EVFTA |
| Export to USA | N/A | Tariff risk (~$12+/unit) |
| Strategic risk | High labour cost | Trade tension/tariffs |

## Key Strategic Insight
- Atlanta = efficient for premium/US market production (learning curve advantage)
- Vietnam = essential for cost-competitive Asian market + European export via EVFTA
- Expanding Vietnam capacity NOW is critical — 2-year lead time means capacity arrives at peak demand
- Contract manufacturing can bridge short-term gaps but at 15-20% premium with no IP/learning benefit
