## Distinction Level (85-100%) Requirements

### Content Quality
- Outstanding command of international business strategy applied SPECIFICALLY to Mobilé Inc.
- Three-wave structure fully understood and integrated throughout EVERY task
- Exceptional ability to analyse, synthesise, and evaluate across multiple analytical frameworks with genuine strategic insight
- Evidence of extensive reading beyond course material, including relevant academic and industry sources

### Strategic Recommendations
- Fully specific, clearly differentiated by market and technology wave
- Addresses trade-offs with honesty (including $90M cash reserve constraint, simultaneous capital demands)
- Grounded in case evidence throughout
- Reflects sophisticated understanding of corporate responsibility in a global, multi-market context

### Strategic Direction (Task 1)
- Outstandingly coherent: vision, mission, goals, SMART objectives interlock seamlessly
- Reflects a deliberate, well-considered stance on the three-wave resource allocation challenge
- Genuine independent thought beyond course material

### Presentation
- Writing is fluent, clear, concise, grammatically correct
- Tables, diagrams, and figures are highly effective in supporting text and presenting key messages
- Comprehensive, error-free citations and reference list in Harvard style

## Task-Specific Scoring Details

### Executive Summary (5 marks)
- Must capture: (a) core strategic direction in context of three-wave structure, (b) most critical internal/external findings including capability gaps, (c) synthesised SWOT position and primary strategic implication, (d) recommended strategic choice including how Mobilé manages 4G, 5G, and AI waves simultaneously
- A board member should be able to read this independently
- Marks for precision and selectivity, NOT length or exhaustive listing
- NOT a repetition of task-by-task outputs — it is the confident conclusion a senior decision-maker needs to act

### Strategy Process (15 marks)
Assessed across four elements:
1. **Vision:** Reflects three-wave nature? Ambitious yet grounded in actual competitive situation post-DOJ?
2. **Mission:** Articulates what Mobilé does, for whom, in which markets? Specific to its global, multi-market, multi-technology portfolio?
3. **Strategic Goals:** Substantively linked to three waves? Balance 4G defense, 5G first-mover, AI R&D commitment?
4. **SMART Objectives:** Specific with quantified targets traceable to case data? Measurable, realistic, time-bound? Internally consistent across goal hierarchy?

Generic statements like "to be the leading smartphone company globally" = minimal credit.
Top marks require every element reinforcing the others, reflecting deliberate three-wave stance (McKinsey three horizon framework).

### Internal & External Analysis (30 marks) — HIGHEST WEIGHTED
**Internal Analysis:**
- Apply VRIO/RBV framework, Value Chain Analysis
- Go BEYOND listing generic resources
- Identify SPECIFIC capabilities: Atlanta manufacturing network, 4G cash generation engine, existing R&D infrastructure, management experience across three global markets
- Explain WHY each represents competitive advantage or strategic vulnerability in the 2025 context

**External Analysis:**
- Apply PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, Strategic Group Analysis, CSF
- Map forces RESHAPING Mobilé's landscape: DOJ intervention ending tacit price coordination, 5G Sub-6GHz coverage exceeding 70% in USA, consumer appetite for AI-powered use cases, competitor R&D timelines, structural dynamics of competing against three equally-sized international rivals
- Framework JUSTIFICATION required: explain why each framework was selected and what specific insights it generates

**Red flags (limited marks):**
- Descriptive or template-filling application of frameworks
- Generic analysis without genuine case-specific interpretation
- No framework justification

### SWOT Synthesis (15 marks)
Three tests every SWOT point must meet:
1. **Evidenced:** Traces directly to Task 2 findings. No new information.
2. **Case-specific:** Specific to Mobilé's actual situation (e.g., "No 5G handset despite Sub-6GHz coverage exceeding 70% of US urban populations" as a weakness)
3. **Strategically interpreted:** Each point carries an explicit implication for strategic direction

Must also:
- Make internal-external connections visible (e.g., manufacturing capability strength → 5G market opportunity)
- Connect 4G revenue dependence weakness → post-DOJ price competition threat
- Be logically consistent with Task 4 recommendation
- NOT simply list generic points

### Strategic Choice & Recommendation (15 marks)
Assessed across five dimensions:
1. **Clarity:** Clear, unambiguous position — which markets to prioritise, which waves to invest in, competitive posture to adopt
2. **Three-wave coherence:** Explicitly addresses how all three waves managed simultaneously (4G protection, 5G deployment, AI R&D)
3. **Analytical consistency:** Follows from and is consistent with Tasks 1-3. Contradictions = mark deduction.
4. **Trade-off honesty:** Acknowledges $90M cash floor, simultaneous capital demands, 5G launch + AI R&D tension
5. **Executive communication:** Clear, specific, actionable strategic brief — NOT a summary of analysis. Make the decision, justify it, explain what it requires.

Vague or hedged recommendations = low marks.
