# The Three Waves of Value — Detailed Analysis

## Framework: McKinsey Three Horizons
The three waves map directly to McKinsey's Three Horizons framework:
- Horizon 1 (H1): Core business generating today's cash — 4G LTE
- Horizon 2 (H2): Emerging opportunity requiring immediate investment — 5G
- Horizon 3 (H3): Future growth requiring early-stage R&D — AI-integrated devices

## Wave 1: 4G LTE — "The Cash Engine"

### Current State
- Technology status: Mature, full production
- Feature level: 6/10 (mid-to-premium)
- Revenue: ~$4.8B annually — 100% of current revenue
- Market position: Strong but eroding

### Strategic Imperatives
- **Defend:** Protect market share and cash generation
- **Extract:** Maximize cash to fund Waves 2 and 3
- **Manage decline:** 4G's relevance shortens as 5G networks expand
- **Cost efficiency:** Critical in price-sensitive Asian emerging markets

### Key Risks
- Post-DOJ price competition eroding margins
- Rivals cutting prices aggressively
- Treating 4G as permanent rather than depleting asset
- Over-investment in defending 4G at expense of 5G transition

## Wave 2: 5G Sub-6GHz — "The Urgent Transition"

### Current State
- Technology status: Surging, mainstream adoption
- Network coverage: 70%+ of US urban areas + major European cities
- Consumer adoption: 38% US, 28% EU (by end-2024)
- Mobilé's position: **Has NOT yet launched a 5G handset** — critical competitive gap

### Why Urgency Matters
- First-mover in 5G brand positioning → pricing premium + loyalty advantage
- Later entrants find this advantage difficult to erode
- The second wave is already breaking — question is readiness
- AI-powered use cases accelerating consumer appetite beyond forecasts

### Technology Choice
| Option | Speed | IP Ownership | Cost | Risk |
|--------|-------|-------------|------|------|
| In-house R&D | Slower | Builds proprietary IP | Lower long-term | Miss the window |
| Licensing | Immediate | No IP ownership | Costly upfront | Dependency on licensor |

### Demand Drivers
- Real-time language translation
- Cloud gaming
- AR navigation
- Seamless remote work
- Carrier subsidies accelerating adoption

## Wave 3: AI-Integrated Devices — "The Future Bet"

### Current State
- Technology status: Emerging, 2027 horizon
- On-device AI chips enabling: real-time photography enhancement, health monitoring, personalised AI assistants, language processing — all without cloud dependency
- Commercial availability projected: 2027-2028

### Competitive Landscape
- Apple, Samsung, Qualcomm actively developing
- Revenue 3-4 years away
- R&D decisions made in 2025 determine leader vs follower

### The Window
- Open NOW in 2025
- Will NOT remain open indefinitely
- Firms waiting until Wave 2 is profitable before investing → permanently forfeit technology leadership
- Building inimitable AI-device capability requires early commitment

## The Interdependence
```
Wave 1 (4G) ──generates cash──→ funds Wave 2 (5G) investment
Wave 1 (4G) ──generates cash──→ funds Wave 3 (AI) R&D
Wave 2 (5G) ──if delayed──→ competitors capture premium positioning
Wave 3 (AI) ──if delayed──→ permanent technology follower status
Wave 2 + 3 ──simultaneous demand──→ cash flow pressure on Wave 1
```

## Resource Allocation Tension
- Cannot maximize all three simultaneously (finite resources)
- Cannot ignore any one (each has irreversible consequences for delay)
- Must sequence investments coherently across all three time horizons
- $90M cash floor constrains total simultaneous investment capacity
