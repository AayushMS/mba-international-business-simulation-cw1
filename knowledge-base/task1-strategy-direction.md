# Task 1: Strategy Process — Strategic Direction for Mobilé Inc.

## Task Requirement
Define where Mobilé Inc. is going and what it stands for. Develop a vision, mission, strategic goals, and SMART objectives grounded in the three-wave situation.

## Vision Statement
"To lead the global smartphone industry through the 4G-to-5G transition and into the AI-device era by 2028, delivering differentiated technology across the US, European, and Asian markets while sustaining financial resilience through disciplined multi-horizon resource allocation."

### Why this vision works for Mobilé Inc.:
- Reflects the three-wave structure (4G transition, 5G, AI-device)
- Sets a specific time horizon (2028 — aligns with AI-device commercial availability)
- Acknowledges the multi-market, multi-technology nature of the business
- Includes the financial discipline constraint (resource allocation)
- Grounded in the company's actual competitive situation post-DOJ

## Mission Statement
"Mobilé Inc. designs, manufactures, and delivers smartphones that serve the distinct needs of consumers across the United States, Europe, and Asia — defending our established 4G leadership in price-sensitive markets, launching competitive 5G devices for brand-conscious and feature-driven segments, and investing in AI-integrated device R&D to secure long-term technology leadership — all while maintaining the financial discipline required to fund three simultaneous technology waves from a single, finite resource base."

### Why this mission works:
- Articulates WHAT Mobilé does (designs, manufactures, delivers smartphones)
- Specifies FOR WHOM (consumers across three distinct markets)
- Specifies HOW it differentiates across waves (defend 4G, launch 5G, invest AI)
- Acknowledges the central constraint (single resource base, three waves)
- Case-specific — not applicable to any generic technology company

## Strategic Goals (linked to Three Waves)

### Goal 1: Defend 4G Revenue Base (Wave 1)
Protect and optimize the 4G LTE business as the primary cash engine funding all strategic investments, while managing its controlled transition as 5G adoption accelerates.

### Goal 2: Achieve Competitive 5G Market Entry (Wave 2)
Launch a competitive 5G handset range and establish premium brand positioning in the US and European markets before the first-mover window closes.

### Goal 3: Secure AI-Device Technology Position (Wave 3)
Commit to AI-integrated device R&D to ensure Mobilé Inc. is a technology leader, not a follower, when the AI-device market materializes in 2027-2028.

### Goal 4: Maintain Financial Resilience (Cross-Wave)
Allocate resources across all three waves without breaching the $90M cash reserve floor or triggering emergency borrowing.

### Goal 5: Optimize Global Production for Multi-Wave Demand (Cross-Wave)
Expand and reconfigure manufacturing capacity to serve 4G defense, 5G launch, and AI-device production across all three markets.

## SMART Objectives

### Objective 1 (Wave 1 — 4G Defense):
"Maintain at least 22% market share in the Asian 4G mass-market segment through Q4 2026, while reducing per-unit production costs by 8% via Vietnam plant capacity expansion, ensuring annual 4G revenue does not fall below $4.0 billion."
- **Specific:** Asian market, 4G segment, Vietnam expansion
- **Measurable:** 22% share, 8% cost reduction, $4.0B revenue floor
- **Achievable:** Vietnam plant expansion is within capability; 22% is modest decline from 25%
- **Relevant:** Directly protects the cash engine funding Waves 2 and 3
- **Time-bound:** Q4 2026

### Objective 2 (Wave 2 — 5G Launch):
"Launch Mobilé Inc.'s first 5G handset in the US and European premium segments by Q2 2026, targeting 10% 5G market share in the US and 8% in Europe within 12 months of launch."
- **Specific:** US + Europe, premium segment, first 5G product
- **Measurable:** 10% US, 8% EU 5G share
- **Achievable:** 5G technology available via licensing (fast) or in-house (slower)
- **Relevant:** Closes the critical competitive gap (W1 in SWOT)
- **Time-bound:** Launch Q2 2026, share targets by Q2 2027

### Objective 3 (Wave 3 — AI R&D):
"Allocate a minimum of $150M to AI-integrated device R&D by end of FY2026, establishing an internal AI chip development team and securing at least two strategic technology partnerships, with a working prototype targeted for Q4 2027."
- **Specific:** AI R&D budget, team formation, partnerships, prototype
- **Measurable:** $150M investment, 2 partnerships, 1 prototype
- **Achievable:** Requires significant but feasible portion of cash flow
- **Relevant:** Secures Wave 3 position before window closes
- **Time-bound:** FY2026 for investment, Q4 2027 for prototype

### Objective 4 (Financial Discipline):
"Maintain cash reserves above $120M throughout the 2025-2027 planning period (30% buffer above the $90M floor), limiting long-term borrowing to no more than $200M at the standard 4.8% rate, with zero emergency borrowing."
- **Specific:** Cash floor with buffer, borrowing limits
- **Measurable:** $120M minimum, $200M borrowing cap, zero emergency
- **Achievable:** Requires careful sequencing of investments
- **Relevant:** The binding financial constraint of the case
- **Time-bound:** 2025-2027 planning period

### Objective 5 (Production Expansion):
"Commission 4 additional production lines at the Vietnam facility by Q1 2026, increasing total Asian capacity by 33%, while evaluating contract manufacturing for 5G launch bridging at no more than 18% cost premium."
- **Specific:** Vietnam expansion, 4 lines, contract manufacturing for 5G bridge
- **Measurable:** 4 lines, 33% capacity increase, 18% cost cap
- **Achievable:** Within investment capability; 2-year lead time means capacity arrives 2028
- **Relevant:** Addresses capacity needs for all three waves
- **Time-bound:** Q1 2026 commissioning decision
