# Task 2: Internal & External Analysis — Summary

## Task Requirement
Analyse Mobilé Inc.'s internal and external strategic environment. Select appropriate frameworks, justify choices, and explain what each reveals specific to Mobilé's situation.

## Frameworks Selected and Justification

### Internal Analysis
1. **VRIO/RBV Framework** — Selected because the post-DOJ competitive shift demands understanding which of Mobilé's resources can sustain advantage vs. which are merely at parity. See: `frameworks/vrio-analysis.md`

2. **Value Chain Analysis** — Selected because Mobilé's dual-plant manufacturing structure, JIT system, and R&D decisions are central to the case. Value Chain reveals where differential value is created and where operational bottlenecks constrain strategy. See: `frameworks/value-chain.md`

### External Analysis
3. **PESTLE Analysis** — Selected because the DOJ intervention (political/legal), trade tensions (political/economic), 5G rollout (technological), and sustainability regulations (environmental/legal) are all reshaping Mobilé's environment. See: `frameworks/pestle.md`

4. **Porter's Five Forces** — Selected because the DOJ intervention fundamentally altered competitive rivalry, and understanding buyer power, supplier dynamics, and substitute threats is essential for pricing and positioning strategy. See: `frameworks/porters-five-forces.md`

5. **Strategic Group Analysis** — Selected because all four firms currently occupy identical positions but will diverge based on their 5G/AI investment choices. Reveals where Mobilé should position itself as groups separate. See: `frameworks/strategic-group.md`

6. **Critical Success Factors** — Selected to prioritize what must go right across all three waves, directly informing resource allocation. Identifies the most critical gaps (5G product absence, AI R&D commitment). See: `frameworks/csf.md`

## Key Internal Findings
- Atlanta plant's learning-curve efficiencies = temporary competitive advantage (VRIO)
- Vietnam plant's cost advantage is location-based (shared with Samsung, Intel, LG), not proprietary (VRIO)
- Existing R&D is a foundation but NOT yet directed toward 5G or AI (Value Chain + VRIO)
- 4G cash engine is valuable but declining — competitive parity, not advantage (VRIO)
- JIT production efficient for 4G but risky for uncertain 5G demand (Value Chain)
- Dual-plant structure enables market-optimized production strategy (Value Chain)

## Key External Findings
- DOJ intervention is the single most impactful macro force — ended price coordination (PESTLE)
- 5G infrastructure is ready (70%+ US coverage) — Mobilé's product gap is the bottleneck, not technology (PESTLE)
- Competitive rivalry is HIGH and intensifying — four equal firms in direct conflict (Porter's)
- AI-device window is open but competitors (Apple, Samsung, Qualcomm) are already active (PESTLE + CSF)
- Asian market growth (22-30%) is the highest opportunity but requires bifurcated strategy (PESTLE + Porter's)
- Strategic group positions are still fluid — no firm has permanently captured 5G first-mover status (Strategic Group)
- $90M cash floor + emergency borrowing risk constrains investment ambition (PESTLE + CSF)

## Cross-Cutting Insight
The internal-external alignment reveals one central theme: **Mobilé's internal capabilities (manufacturing, R&D base, global presence) are sufficient to compete, but they are misaligned with the external reality (5G demand, AI R&D race, post-DOJ competition).** The strategic task is REALIGNMENT — redirecting existing capabilities toward the most critical external opportunities (5G launch, AI R&D) while defending the cash base (4G) that funds everything.
