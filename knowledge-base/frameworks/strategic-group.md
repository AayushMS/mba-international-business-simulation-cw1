# Strategic Group Analysis — Mobilé Inc.

## Framework Purpose
Strategic Group Analysis maps competitors based on shared strategic characteristics, revealing competitive positioning and mobility barriers within the smartphone industry.

**Why Strategic Group for Mobilé Inc.:** All four firms currently occupy identical positions (25% share, same markets, same technology). The DOJ intervention removes the coordination that kept them together. Strategic Group analysis reveals how firms will likely diverge — and where Mobilé should position itself.

## Current Strategic Groups (Pre-Divergence, 2025)

### Mapping Dimensions
- **X-axis:** Technology breadth (4G only → 4G + 5G → 4G + 5G + AI pipeline)
- **Y-axis:** Geographic market focus (single market → dual market → full global)

### Current Position (All Four Firms)
- All four firms currently cluster in one group: "Global 4G Incumbents"
- 25% share each, all three markets, 4G-only product lines
- This is UNSTABLE — the cluster will break apart as firms make different 5G/AI investment choices

## Projected Strategic Group Migration (2025-2028)

### Group A: "5G First-Movers" (Target Position for Mobilé)
- Firms that launch 5G handsets first
- Capture premium pricing, brand loyalty, carrier partnerships
- Command price premiums in USA and Europe
- Mobility barrier to entry: Time advantage, brand positioning, carrier relationships

### Group B: "4G Defenders"
- Firms that prioritize 4G cash defense over 5G investment
- Strong in Asian mass-market (price-competitive 4G)
- Risk: Becoming technology followers as 5G adoption accelerates
- Mobility barrier to exit: Sunk costs in 4G production, declining but still profitable revenue

### Group C: "Technology Leaders"
- Firms that invest heavily in both 5G AND AI R&D
- Positioned for Wave 3 dominance (2027-28)
- Requires significant upfront investment with delayed returns
- Mobility barrier: R&D investment, IP portfolio, talent acquisition

### Group D: "Fast Followers"
- Firms that license 5G technology rather than developing in-house
- Quick to market but no IP advantage
- Dependent on licensors, vulnerable to cost pressure
- Low mobility barriers — easy to enter, easy to be displaced

## Mobilé Inc.'s Strategic Choice
The case study fundamentally asks: **which strategic group should Mobilé Inc. target?**

**Recommended trajectory:** Move from "Global 4G Incumbent" → "5G First-Mover" while investing in AI R&D to eventually join "Technology Leader" group.

This requires:
1. Rapid 5G launch (closing the critical competitive gap)
2. Simultaneous AI R&D investment (securing future position)
3. Managed 4G decline (extracting cash while defending share)

## SWOT Feed
- **Opportunities:** No firm has yet established permanent 5G brand leadership — strategic group positions are still fluid
- **Threats:** If competitors reach "5G First-Mover" group first, mobility barriers make catching up expensive and slow
