# VRIO Analysis — Mobilé Inc.

## Framework Purpose
VRIO (Value, Rarity, Imitability, Organisation) identifies which of Mobilé Inc.'s resources and capabilities represent genuine sources of competitive advantage vs. strategic vulnerabilities in the post-DOJ 2025 landscape.

**Why VRIO for Mobilé Inc.:** The case demands understanding which internal resources can sustain competitive advantage as the industry shifts from tacit coordination to open competition. Mobilé needs to know what it can leverage across all three technology waves.

## Key Findings

### Sources of Potential Advantage
1. **Atlanta learning-curve efficiencies** — years of accumulated production optimisation create a cost-quality advantage for premium products that rivals would need time to replicate
2. **Existing R&D infrastructure** — if rapidly redirected toward 5G and AI, could become a source of advantage. Currently underutilised for next-gen technology.
3. **Multi-market management experience** — the ability to coordinate strategy across three distinct markets while managing three technology waves simultaneously is a complex capability

### Strategic Vulnerabilities
1. **No 5G capability** — the most critical gap. Every competitor is racing to fill this. Mobilé has zero 5G product to show.
2. **4G revenue dependency** — 100% of revenue from a depleting technology wave is a fundamental vulnerability
3. **Vietnam plant immaturity** — still growing, not yet at efficiency parity with Atlanta. Cost advantage is location-based (accessible to all), not capability-based.

### SWOT Feed
- **Strengths:** Atlanta manufacturing efficiency, existing R&D base, global market presence
- **Weaknesses:** No 5G product, total 4G revenue dependency, immature Vietnam operations
