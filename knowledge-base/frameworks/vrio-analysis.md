# VRIO Analysis — Mobilé Inc.

## Framework Purpose
VRIO (Value, Rarity, Imitability, Organisation) identifies which of Mobilé Inc.'s resources and capabilities represent genuine sources of competitive advantage vs. strategic vulnerabilities in the post-DOJ 2025 landscape.

**Why VRIO for Mobilé Inc.:** The case demands understanding which internal resources can sustain competitive advantage as the industry shifts from tacit coordination to open competition. Mobilé needs to know what it can leverage across all three technology waves.

## Key Findings

### Sources of Potential Advantage
1. **Atlanta learning-curve efficiencies** — years of accumulated production optimisation create a cost-quality advantage for premium products that rivals would need time to replicate. Atlanta's 12 lines producing 4G at feature level 6 (mid-to-premium) are optimised for this segment.
2. **Existing R&D infrastructure** — if rapidly redirected toward 5G and AI, could become a source of advantage. Currently underutilised for next-gen technology. The QP specifically identifies this as a named capability requiring analysis.
3. **Multi-market management experience** — the ability to coordinate strategy across three distinct markets (US: brand-conscious; EU: feature-conscious, sustainability-aware; Asia: bifurcated) while managing three technology waves simultaneously is a complex, tacit capability

### Strategic Vulnerabilities
1. **No 5G capability** — the most critical gap. Every competitor is racing to fill this. Mobilé has zero 5G product despite 70%+ US urban 5G coverage and 38% US consumer 5G ownership.
2. **4G revenue dependency** — 100% of $4.8B annual revenue from a single depleting technology wave (feature level 6, mature) is a fundamental vulnerability
3. **Vietnam plant immaturity** — still growing, not yet at efficiency parity with Atlanta. Cost advantage is location-based (accessible to all — Samsung, Intel, LG also operate in Vietnam), not capability-based.

### Framework Verdict: Mobilé's Competitive Position
Applying VRIO rigorously: Mobilé currently holds **no sustained competitive advantages** — only temporary advantages (Atlanta learning curve) and competitive parity positions. The critical strategic task is converting the potential advantage of the R&D infrastructure into actual 5G and AI capabilities before competitors complete their own transitions.

### SWOT Feed
- **Strengths:** Atlanta manufacturing efficiency (temporary advantage), existing R&D base (potential advantage if redirected), global market presence (parity)
- **Weaknesses:** No 5G product (most critical), total 4G revenue dependency, immature Vietnam operations (parity, not advantage)
