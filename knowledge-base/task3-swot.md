## Summary for Presentation

### Strengths
1. Dual-plant manufacturing with cost differentiation (Atlanta premium + Vietnam cost-competitive)
2. Established 4G cash generation engine ($4.8B annually)
3. Existing R&D infrastructure and global management experience
4. JIT production and operational efficiency

### Weaknesses
1. **No 5G handset product** — the most critical gap
2. Total revenue dependency on depleting 4G technology
3. Vietnam plant still building maturity and efficiency
4. R&D not yet directed toward 5G or AI

### Opportunities
1. 5G infrastructure ready — market waiting for Mobilé's product
2. AI-device market window open for early movers (2025-2028)
3. Asian market explosive growth (22-30% p.a.)
4. EVFTA trade route and European sustainability differentiation
5. No competitor has permanently captured 5G first-mover position

### Threats
1. Post-DOJ price competition compressing margins
2. Competitors may capture 5G first-mover position before Mobilé
3. Vietnam plant tariff and trade risk ($12+/unit)
4. Apple/Samsung/Qualcomm already active in AI R&D
5. Simultaneous investment demands risk breaching $90M cash floor

## Primary Strategic Implication
The SWOT reveals a company with **sufficient internal capabilities that are misaligned with external reality**. The dominant strategic move is clear: close the 5G gap immediately (W1 → O1) while committing to AI R&D (W4 → O2), funded by defending but not over-investing in the 4G cash engine (S2 → managing T1). All within the $90M cash floor constraint (T5).

This sets up the Task 4 recommendation.
