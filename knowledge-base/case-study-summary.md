# Mobilé Inc. Case Study Summary

## Company Overview
- US-based mobile handset manufacturer
- Operations spanning three global markets: USA (home), Europe, Asia
- Designs, manufactures, and sells smartphones across technology generations and price tiers
- Competes against three other major international players of broadly similar size and capability
- All four firms start from identical 25% market share in all three markets

## The 2025 Context
- DOJ dismantled tacit price coordination arrangements in early 2025
- All four firms now in open, direct competition for the first time
- Every firm simultaneously racing to: launch 5G devices, invest in AI-integrated handsets, undercut rivals on price, secure manufacturing capacity
- Mobile industry experiencing most turbulent competitive period since 2007 smartphone revolution

## The Three Waves of Value (McKinsey Three Horizons)

### Wave 1: 4G LTE (Horizon 1 — Defend & Extract)
- **Status:** Mature, full production
- **Revenue:** ~$4.8 billion annually (entire current revenue)
- **Feature level:** 6 (mid-to-premium)
- **Strategic role:** Core cash engine funding Waves 2 and 3
- **Growth:** Through upgrades and emerging markets (Asia)
- **Threat:** Competitive moat eroding as rivals cut prices post-DOJ
- **Danger:** Treating this as permanent rather than depleting asset

### Wave 2: 5G Sub-6GHz (Horizon 2 — Launch Urgently)
- **Status:** Surging, mainstream now
- **Network coverage:** 70%+ of US urban areas, major European cities
- **Consumer adoption:** 38% US, 28% EU already own 5G device
- **Critical gap:** Mobilé Inc. has NOT yet launched a 5G handset
- **First-mover advantage:** Material and time-limited — premium 5G brand positioning is at stake
- **Technology options:** In-house R&D (slower, builds IP) OR licensing (immediate, costly, no IP)
- **AI-powered use cases driving demand:** Real-time language translation, cloud gaming, AR navigation, seamless remote work

### Wave 3: AI-Integrated Devices (Horizon 3 — Invest Now for 2027)
- **Status:** Emerging, 2027 horizon
- **What it is:** Smartphones with on-device AI chips for real-time photography, health monitoring, personalised AI assistants, language processing
- **Competitors active:** Apple, Samsung, Qualcomm — commercial availability 2027-28
- **Revenue timeline:** 3-4 years away
- **Critical decision:** R&D investment NOW determines whether Mobilé leads or follows
- **Window:** Open in 2025, will NOT remain open indefinitely
- **Risk of delay:** Permanently forfeit technology leadership

### Wave 4: 6G (NOT in scope)
- Research phase, 2030+
- Government-funded research active globally
- Theoretical 1 Tbps speeds
- Foundational R&D now builds positioning for 6G era

## The Central Strategic Tension
- Wave 1 generates the cash
- Wave 2 demands it immediately
- Wave 3 requires investment before generating any return
- Maximizing Wave 1 at Wave 2's expense → technology follower
- Abandoning Wave 1 too quickly → starves cash for Wave 3
- Must hold all three in view at once with coherent resource allocation across all time horizons

## Five Simultaneous Pressures
1. Defending 4G market share vs aggressive pricing (especially Asia) — volume + cost efficiency = survival
2. Launching 5G before first-mover premium window closes — every delay round cedes pricing power
3. Investing in AI-device R&D now for 2027 market — delay = permanently following, not leading
4. Expanding Asian production capacity (2-year lead time) — capacity committed now arrives at peak demand
5. Maintaining financial discipline — simultaneous R&D, capacity, marketing creates cash flow pressure
