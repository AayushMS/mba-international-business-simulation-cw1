# Task 4: Strategic Choice & Recommendation

## Task Requirement
Make a clear strategic recommendation for Mobilé Inc. Explain where and how the company should compete, prioritise across its three waves, and manage key risks. Must align with Tasks 1-3. Executive-level communication.

---

## THE RECOMMENDATION

### Strategic Choice: "Accelerated 5G Entry with Parallel AI R&D, Funded by Managed 4G Transition"

Mobilé Inc. should pursue a **sequenced dual-investment strategy**: launch 5G via technology licensing within 6-9 months to close the critical competitive gap, while simultaneously committing to in-house AI-device R&D to secure long-term technology leadership — all funded by disciplined management of the 4G cash engine with targeted cost reductions.

---

## Wave-by-Wave Action Plan

### Wave 1: 4G — "Defend, Extract, Don't Over-Invest"
**What:** Protect 4G revenue as the cash engine, but accept managed decline. Do NOT pour incremental investment into a depleting asset.

**Specific actions:**
- Maintain 4G production at Atlanta (premium US/EU) and expand Vietnam (cost-competitive Asia)
- Target cost reduction of 8% per unit via Vietnam scale-up
- Accept gradual market share erosion (25% → 22% target floor) as a deliberate trade-off — resources freed fund Waves 2 and 3
- Price aggressively in Asian mass-market to maintain volume (Vietnam cost advantage)
- Optimize, don't maximize — extract cash, don't defend every point of share

**Why:** 4G funds everything. But over-defending a mature technology at the expense of 5G launch is the single biggest strategic mistake Mobilé can make.

### Wave 2: 5G — "License to Launch Fast, Build IP in Parallel"
**What:** License 5G technology for an immediate market entry (6-9 months), while beginning in-house 5G R&D for second-generation products that build proprietary IP.

**Specific actions:**
- License 5G chipset technology from an established supplier for first-generation 5G handset
- Target US and European premium segments first (brand-conscious, feature-driven, 5G-ready infrastructure)
- Launch by Q2 2026 — every quarter of delay cedes first-mover advantage
- Begin in-house 5G R&D immediately — second-gen 5G product (12-18 months post-launch) transitions to proprietary technology
- Use contract manufacturing (15-20% premium) to bridge production gap while 5G-capable lines are established
- Target 10% US / 8% EU 5G market share within 12 months of launch

**Why licensing first:**
- Speed is the critical factor — the first-mover window is closing
- Licensing gets a product to market immediately
- In-house R&D alone is too slow — competitors are already launching
- The licensing cost is an acceptable price for capturing premium 5G brand positioning

**Why in-house R&D in parallel:**
- Licensing creates supplier dependency (Porter's: supplier power)
- Proprietary IP is a sustainable competitive advantage (VRIO: costly to imitate)
- Second-gen 5G product with proprietary technology differentiates from other licensees

### Wave 3: AI-Devices — "Invest Now, Small but Committed"
**What:** Commit a meaningful but bounded R&D budget to AI-integrated device development. Do not wait for 5G to become profitable first — the window closes.

**Specific actions:**
- Allocate $150M to AI R&D over FY2025-2026
- Establish dedicated AI device team (separate from 5G team)
- Secure 2 strategic technology partnerships (chip design, AI software)
- Target working prototype by Q4 2027
- Focus on on-device AI capabilities: photography, health monitoring, personalised assistants
- Position for commercial launch in 2028 alongside or ahead of competitors

**Why commit now despite no immediate revenue:**
- Apple, Samsung, Qualcomm are all active — delay = permanent follower status (CSF analysis)
- The investment window is open in 2025 but will not remain open indefinitely
- $150M is significant but manageable within the cash constraint if 4G revenue is defended

## Financial Discipline & Risk Management

### Cash Constraint Management
- Maintain $120M minimum (30% buffer above $90M floor)
- Fund 5G licensing from current 4G cash flow (~$4.8B revenue base)
- Fund AI R&D from allocated budget ($150M over 2 years)
- Use long-term borrowing if needed (4.8% rate), NEVER trigger emergency borrowing (7.8% + investor damage)
- Sequence investments: 5G licensing costs are front-loaded; AI R&D ramps gradually

### Key Risks Acknowledged
1. **5G licensing dependency** — mitigated by parallel in-house R&D for second-gen product
2. **4G revenue decline faster than expected** — mitigated by aggressive cost reduction and Vietnam expansion
3. **Cash floor breach risk** — mitigated by $120M buffer and investment sequencing
4. **AI R&D may not yield competitive product** — mitigated by partnership strategy (risk-sharing)
5. **Vietnam tariff escalation** — mitigated by maintaining Atlanta capacity for US market

### Trade-Off Honesty
- We CANNOT do everything simultaneously at maximum scale — investments must be sequenced
- 4G market share will decline — this is a deliberate choice, not a failure
- Licensing 5G is faster but creates short-term supplier dependency
- AI R&D investment has uncertain returns 3-4 years out
- The $90M floor means we cannot pursue all opportunities at their theoretical maximum
