## Project Overview
MBA coursework (MN7002NI) — International Business Strategy with Simulation. Building an interactive React+Vite website as a research & prep tool for a 30-slide group presentation analyzing Mobilé Inc.'s strategic position in 2025.

**Deadline:** 10 April 2026 | **Weight:** 30% of module grade | **Format:** PDF of max 30 slides

## The Case
Mobilé Inc. is a US smartphone manufacturer competing in USA, Europe, and Asia. Post-DOJ intervention (early 2025) ending tacit price coordination, the company faces three simultaneous technology waves:
- **Wave 1 (4G LTE):** Mature cash engine, ~$4.8B revenue, under price pressure
- **Wave 2 (5G):** Urgent — 70%+ urban coverage, Mobilé has NOT launched a 5G handset yet
- **Wave 3 (AI-devices):** 2027-28 horizon, R&D investment decisions must happen NOW

**Central constraint:** USD 90M minimum cash reserve floor. All three waves compete for the same finite resource pool.

## Critical Rules for All Agents

### Content Rules
1. **CASE-SPECIFIC ONLY.** Every analytical point must trace to specific Mobilé Inc. data from the case study. Generic statements like "to be the leading smartphone company globally" score zero.
2. **THREE-WAVE COHERENCE.** Every section must acknowledge the simultaneous 4G/5G/AI tension. This is THE defining challenge of the case.
3. **TASKS BUILD ON EACH OTHER.** Task 3 (SWOT) must only contain evidence from Task 2. Task 4 must align with Tasks 1-3. No contradictions between tasks.
4. **NO NEW INFORMATION IN SWOT.** Every SWOT point must trace to Task 2 findings. No unsupported claims.
5. **HARVARD REFERENCING.** All citations must use Harvard format.
6. **EXECUTIVE COMMUNICATION.** Task 4 recommendation must be clear, specific, actionable — not a summary of analysis.

## Key Data Points (Quick Reference)

- Asia: ~1B users, bifurcated (premium 5G + mass 4G), 22-30% growth
- 5G adoption: 38% US, 28% EU by end-2024
- AI-device commercial availability: 2027-28
- New plant lead time: 2 years from investment to operational
- Overproduction penalty: 5-10% cost; Underproduction = permanently lost sales
