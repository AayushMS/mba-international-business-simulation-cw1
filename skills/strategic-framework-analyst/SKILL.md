# Strategic Framework Analyst — Mobilé Inc.

This skill ensures strategic frameworks are applied with genuine case-specific insight, not as template-filling exercises. The marking criteria is explicit: "High marks are reserved for analysis that produces actionable strategic insight rather than a completed checklist."

## Framework Selection & Justification

The following frameworks have been selected for Mobilé Inc. Each has a specific justification:

### Internal Analysis Frameworks

**VRIO/Resource-Based View**
- *Why for Mobilé:* The post-DOJ shift from tacit coordination to open competition demands understanding which resources sustain advantage vs. which are merely at parity. All four firms start equal (25% share) — VRIO reveals what Mobilé can leverage to differentiate.
- *Key resources to analyse:* Atlanta learning-curve efficiencies, Vietnam low-cost labour, 4G cash engine, existing R&D base, multi-market management experience, JIT capability
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/vrio-analysis.md`

**Value Chain Analysis**
- *Why for Mobilé:* The case provides detailed operational data (dual plants, JIT, production costs, trade routes). Value Chain reveals where differential value is created and where operational bottlenecks constrain the three-wave strategy.
- *Key activities:* Manufacturing (Atlanta vs Vietnam), R&D (not yet directed to 5G/AI), logistics (EVFTA route, tariff risks), marketing (no 5G product to market)
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/value-chain.md`

### External Analysis Frameworks

**PESTLE**
- *Why for Mobilé:* The DOJ intervention (political/legal), trade tensions (political/economic), 5G rollout (technological), and EU sustainability regulations (environmental/legal) are all actively reshaping the competitive landscape.
- *Key factors:* DOJ antitrust action, US-Vietnam tariffs, 5G Sub-6GHz coverage milestones, AI-device development timelines, EU repairability regulations, EVFTA
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/pestle.md`

**Porter's Five Forces**
- *Why for Mobilé:* The DOJ intervention fundamentally altered competitive rivalry — the central force. Understanding how rivalry, buyer power, supplier dynamics, and substitution interact reveals the structural constraints on Mobilé's pricing and positioning.
- *Key dynamics:* Four equally-sized firms in direct conflict, rising buyer power from price transparency, supplier power in 5G licensing decision, intra-category substitution (4G→5G→AI)
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/porters-five-forces.md`

**Strategic Group Analysis**
- *Why for Mobilé:* All four firms currently occupy identical positions but will diverge based on 5G/AI investment choices. Strategic Group mapping reveals the target positioning and the mobility barriers that make early moves defensible.
- *Key insight:* Groups are still fluid — no firm has permanently captured 5G first-mover position
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/strategic-group.md`

**Critical Success Factors**
- *Why for Mobilé:* With five simultaneous pressures competing for finite resources, CSF analysis prioritises what MUST go right vs. what is merely important. Directly informs resource allocation.
- *Key CSFs:* Speed to 5G market (most critical), AI R&D commitment, 4G cash defense, financial discipline ($90M floor), production capacity timing
- *Pre-built analysis:* `knowledge-base/frameworks/csf.md`

## Quality Checklist for Each Framework

Before finalising any framework analysis, verify:

- [ ] **Justification present:** "Why this framework for Mobilé Inc." section exists and is compelling
- [ ] **Case-specific evidence:** Every point cites specific data (numbers, facts, constraints from the case)
- [ ] **No generic template-filling:** Analysis goes beyond listing categories — each point has a "so what?" implication
- [ ] **Three-wave connection:** Each finding is connected to at least one of the three waves (4G/5G/AI)
- [ ] **SWOT feed identified:** Key findings are tagged as feeding into Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, or Threats
- [ ] **Internal-external links:** Where applicable, internal findings connect to external findings (e.g., manufacturing strength → market opportunity)

## SWOT Synthesis Rules

The SWOT is a synthesis document, not a standalone exercise. Three tests for every point:

1. **Evidenced:** Traces directly to a specific framework finding from Task 2. No new information or unsupported claims.
2. **Case-specific:** Would not apply to a generic smartphone company. Must reference Mobilé's actual situation.
3. **Strategically interpreted:** Carries an explicit implication — "What does this S/W/O/T mean for Mobilé's choices?"

Additionally:
- Make **internal-external connections** visible (S1↔O3, W2↔T1)
- Use **wave colour coding**: 4G=blue, 5G=green, AI=purple
- Ensure **logical consistency with Task 4** recommendation — a SWOT that contradicts the recommendation loses marks

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **The Generic PESTLE:** "Political factors affect the business environment" → Instead: "The DOJ antitrust intervention in early 2025 dismantled tacit price coordination among four equal firms, forcing open competition for the first time and compressing margins that previously funded strategic investment."

2. **The Descriptive VRIO:** "The company has manufacturing capabilities" → Instead: "Atlanta's 12-line production facility with years of accumulated learning-curve efficiencies represents a temporary competitive advantage for premium product manufacturing — rivals can invest in similar capacity but cannot immediately replicate the operational knowledge built over years."

3. **The Disconnected SWOT:** Listing strengths that never appear in the recommendation → Every SWOT point should have a clear strategic action implication.

4. **The Unjustified Framework:** Applying Porter's Five Forces without explaining why industry structure analysis matters for Mobilé's specific competitive situation post-DOJ.
